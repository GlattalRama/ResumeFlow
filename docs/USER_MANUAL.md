# ResumeFlow — User Manual

A guide for job seekers using ResumeFlow to build resumes, track applications, and tailor your resume to each job with AI.

> **In a nutshell:** Build one clean **Base Resume**, track the jobs you apply to as **Applications**, and let the AI **tailor** a copy of your resume to each job — then export to PDF, Word, or PowerPoint.

## 1. Getting started

1. Open the app and click **Sign in**.
2. Sign in with your **Google account**. ResumeFlow uses Google to sign you in and to privately store your resumes and applications in your own Google Drive (in a hidden app folder only this app can see).
3. After signing in you land on the **Dashboard**.

The top navigation bar always shows: **Dashboard · Resumes · Applications · Settings**.

---

## 2. The dashboard

The dashboard is your home base. From here you can jump to your **Resumes** and **Applications**, and start a new resume.

---

## 3. Building a resume

Go to **Resumes → + New resume**. You can start two ways:

### Option A — Import from PDF/Word (fastest)
On the New Resume screen, use **Import from PDF/Word**. Upload an existing resume file and the AI reads it and pre-fills your details (name, summary, experience, skills, etc.). Review everything — AI extraction isn't perfect — then adjust.

### Option B — Start from scratch
Fill in the form cards. The live preview on the right updates as you type. The cards include:

- **Basics** — name, title, contact details, and an optional **profile photo** (square or circle).
- **Summary** — a short professional summary. Use the **Improve with AI** button to sharpen the wording.
- **Areas of Expertise** — high-level strengths, shown as bullets.
- **Work Experience** — company, role, dates, location, and achievement bullet points.
- **Education** — school, degree, field, dates.
- **Projects** — notable projects with descriptions.
- **Technical Skills** — grouped as Category / Value rows (e.g. *Languages: Python, Java*).
- **Certifications** and **Languages**.
- **Custom Sections** — add your own sections (free text, bullets, two-column bullets, or category/value).

> **Improve with AI:** Many text fields have an **Improve** button that rewrites the text more clearly. It only rephrases what you wrote — it does not invent facts.

When you're done, **Save**. Your resume appears under **Resumes**.

### Document Sections (reorder, rename, hide)
Open the **Document Sections** card to:
- **Reorder** sections (drag),
- **Rename** a section heading (e.g. "Work Experience" → "Professional Experience"),
- **Hide** a section from the output,
- Add a **page break** after a section.

These choices affect the preview and every export.

## 4. Choosing & customizing a template

In the resume builder:

- **Template** card — pick a layout. Options may include **ATS Corporate Style**, **Cognizant Corporate** (branded layout with a logo, two-column expertise band, and a dedicated projects page), and others your admin has enabled.
- **Font & Colors** card — change the font, base size, accent color, and page margins.
- **ATS-safe mode** (toggle on the resume preview) — renders a single-column, no-photo version that automated applicant-tracking systems parse most reliably. It affects the on-screen preview, the PDF, and the DOCX export.

> Some templates (like **Cognizant Corporate**) use a fixed brand look — their colors/font are set by design, while size, margins, and spacing remain adjustable.

## 5. The Base Resume

Think of the **Base Resume** as your clean **master copy** (like the "main" version everything else branches from).

- On the **Resumes** list (or a resume's page), click **Set as base** to designate one.
- A green **★ Base Resume** badge marks it.
- When you tailor a resume to a job, the Base Resume is the **default source**.

**Important rules:**
- The Base Resume **cannot be deleted** — it protects you from accidentally losing your master copy.
- You **cannot delete any resume until a Base Resume is set**. If you try, you'll be prompted to choose a Base Resume first.
- To delete your current base, **set a different resume as the base first**, then delete the old one.

## 6. Exporting (PDF / Word / PowerPoint)

Open a resume and use the export buttons:

- **Download PDF** — prints exactly what you see in the preview (including your template, colors, and any page breaks). This is the recommended format for applications.
- **Download DOCX** — an editable Word version.
- **Download PPTX** — a PowerPoint version.

> Tip: turn on **ATS-safe mode** before exporting if you're submitting to a company's online application portal.

## 7. Tracking job applications

Go to **Applications → + New application** to add a job you're pursuing. For each application you can record the **company**, **role/job title**, and the **job description**.

> **Paste the job description as text.** The AI features (tailoring, interview prep) read the job description from this text field. A screenshot or PDF of the JD won't work — copy and paste the actual text.

On an application's page you'll find:

- **Status** — move it through the pipeline: *Saved → Applied → Phone Screen → Interview → Offer*, or *Rejected / Withdrawn*. A **status history** is kept automatically.
- **Tailor Resume for this Job** — see the next section.
- **Interview preparation** — AI tools to prep (see [section 9](#9-interview-preparation-ai)).
- **Notes** — free-form notes (recruiter info, follow-ups, to-dos).
- **Documents** — attach related files.

## 8. Tailoring your resume to a job (AI)

This creates a **job-specific copy** of your resume by rephrasing and reordering your existing content to match the job — it **never invents** experience, skills, or numbers.

**Before you start, make sure you have:**
1. **At least one resume created** (ideally a Base Resume), and
2. The **job description pasted as text** into the application.

**Steps:**
1. Open the **application** for the job.
2. Click **Tailor Resume for this Job**.
   - *No resumes yet?* You'll be prompted to **Create resume** first — tailoring needs an existing resume to work from.
3. Choose a **source resume** (defaults to your Base Resume) and click **Generate**.
4. Review **what changed** — each section is labeled *rephrased*, *reordered*, *emphasized*, *unchanged*, or *rejected* (rejected means the AI's suggestion was discarded because it would have added an unverifiable fact).
5. Click **Accept & save as new version** to keep it, or **Regenerate** / **Discard**.

The tailored resume is saved as a **new version** (it does not overwrite your Base Resume), carrying over the source's template and styling.

> **Why tailoring is "truth-preserving":** the app only lets the AI rephrase, reorder, or trim content that already exists in your resume. If a suggestion introduces a new skill, employer, or metric, the app automatically falls back to your original wording.

## 9. Interview preparation (AI)

From an application, open **Interview preparation**. Under **Generate questions** you can run:

- **Generate Interview Q&A** — likely interview questions with suggested answers, drawn from your resume and the job (saved to the Q&A list).
- **Generate Interview Briefing** — a prep briefing for the role.
- **Generate Cover Letter** — a draft cover letter for this job.
- **Generate Follow-up Message** — a post-interview/application follow-up.

Everything is generated from **your resume + this job description** — always review before sending.

## 10. Settings & your AI key

Open **Settings** to configure AI:

- **Model** — which AI model is used for Improve/Tailor/Interview features.
- **Your API key (BYOK – "Bring Your Own Key")** — optional. The app provides a shared key with a **daily free limit**; if you tailor frequently, add your own OpenRouter API key for unlimited use (your key is encrypted and never shown again after saving).

If you hit *"You've used today's free AI suggestions,"* either wait for the daily reset or add your own key here.

## 11. Tips & best practices

- **Build your Base Resume first.** Keep it complete and generic; tailor copies from it per job.
- **Always paste the job description as text** into the application — that's what powers tailoring and interview prep.
- **Tailor, then review.** Skim the "what changed" list and the tailored summary before saving.
- **Use ATS-safe mode** for online application portals; use a styled template (e.g. Cognizant) for direct/email submissions.
- **Keep your achievements specific** (numbers, outcomes) — tailoring can reorder and emphasize them, but it can't invent them.
- **Name tailored versions clearly** — they're auto-named like *"Job Title – Company,"* which keeps your Resumes list organized.

## 12. Troubleshooting & FAQ

**"Tailor Resume for this Job" does nothing / I can't tailor.**
You need at least one resume. Create a resume (and set it as your Base Resume), then try again. The button now opens a prompt with a **Create resume** shortcut when you have none.

**Tailoring runs but barely changes anything.**
The most common cause is an **empty or missing job description**. Open the application and paste the JD **as text** (not an image/PDF). Also note: tailoring intentionally won't add skills or facts that aren't already in your resume.

**"AI suggestions aren't available right now."**
The shared AI key is unavailable or you've hit the daily free limit. Add your own API key in **Settings** for unlimited use, or try again later.

**My profile photo isn't showing in the PDF.**
Make sure the photo uploaded successfully in the **Basics** card, and that **ATS-safe mode** is **off** (ATS-safe mode intentionally removes photos).

**A template I want isn't in the picker.**
Templates can be enabled/disabled by an administrator. Ask your admin to enable it. (Existing resumes always keep rendering their template even if it's later hidden.)

**Where is my data stored?**
Privately in your own Google Drive, in a hidden app-only folder. Your resume content is never used for analytics.
